Best Hotels in Rome for Every Budget in 2026

February 15, 2026 · 6 min read

← Back to Blog

Rome is one of the most visited cities in the world, and for good reason. The Eternal City offers an unparalleled blend of ancient history, world-class art, and some of the best food on the planet. But with thousands of hotels to choose from, finding the right place to stay can be overwhelming.

We've put together this comprehensive guide to help you find the perfect hotel in Rome, whether you're traveling on a tight budget or looking for the ultimate luxury experience.

Where to Stay in Rome: Best Neighborhoods

Before diving into specific hotels, it helps to understand Rome's main neighborhoods for tourists:

  • Centro Storico — The historic heart of Rome, walking distance to the Pantheon, Piazza Navona, and Trevi Fountain. Most convenient but priciest location.
  • Trastevere — Charming cobblestone streets, excellent restaurants, and a lively nightlife scene. Great for travelers who want an authentic Roman experience.
  • Monti — Rome's oldest neighborhood, now a trendy area with vintage shops, wine bars, and a village-like atmosphere near the Colosseum.
  • Testaccio — A local favorite, known for its food market, traditional Roman cuisine, and fewer tourists. Excellent value for money.
  • Prati — Near the Vatican, this elegant residential area offers upscale hotels and easy access to St. Peter's Basilica.

Luxury Hotels ($300+ per night)

For travelers seeking the finest experience Rome has to offer, these luxury hotels deliver impeccable service, stunning locations, and unforgettable ambiance.

Hotel de Russie — Piazza del Popolo

Nestled between Piazza del Popolo and the Spanish Steps, Hotel de Russie is a favorite among discerning travelers. The secret garden and terraced restaurant are highlights, offering a peaceful retreat from the bustling streets. Rooms start around $450 per night in peak season, but the central location and Rocco Forte service make it worth every euro.

Hotel Eden — Via Ludovisi

Recently renovated, Hotel Eden boasts one of the best rooftop restaurants in Rome with panoramic views over the city. The interiors blend classic elegance with modern comfort. Expect to pay $400-$700 per night depending on the season.

Mid-Range Hotels ($120-$300 per night)

You don't need to spend a fortune to enjoy a comfortable and well-located stay in Rome. These mid-range options offer excellent value.

Hotel Raphael — Largo Febo

Just steps from Piazza Navona, Hotel Raphael features an ivy-covered facade and a rooftop terrace with breathtaking views of Rome's skyline. Rooms are elegantly decorated, and the location is unbeatable for sightseeing. Rates typically range from $180-$300 per night.

Chapter Roma — San Lorenzo

A stylish boutique hotel in the vibrant San Lorenzo neighborhood, Chapter Roma combines contemporary design with warm hospitality. The rooftop pool is a huge plus during Roman summers. Rooms start around $150 per night.

Hotel Santa Maria — Trastevere

Set in a former 16th-century cloister, this charming hotel in the heart of Trastevere offers rooms opening onto a beautiful orange tree courtyard. It's one of the best-value stays in a prime location, with rates from $120-$200 per night.

Budget-Friendly Hotels (Under $120 per night)

Rome can absolutely be done on a budget. These affordable hotels prove that you don't need to sacrifice comfort or location.

Hotel Lancelot — Near the Colosseum

A family-run gem just a 5-minute walk from the Colosseum. The Lancelot has been welcoming guests for decades and is known for its friendly staff and home-like atmosphere. Rates start from $90 per night, which is remarkable for the location.

Hotel Artorius — Monti

A clean, modern budget hotel in the trendy Monti neighborhood. Rooms are small but well-designed, and you're steps away from excellent restaurants and the Colosseum. Expect to pay $70-$110 per night.

Tips for Booking Hotels in Rome

  1. Book early for peak season — April to June and September to October are the busiest months. Book at least 2-3 months in advance for the best rates.
  2. Consider shoulder season — November, early March, and late January offer significantly lower prices and fewer crowds.
  3. Check the city tax — Rome charges a tourist tax of $3-7 per person per night depending on the hotel category. This is usually not included in the booking price.
  4. Look for breakfast included — Many Roman hotels include a generous Italian breakfast. This can save you $10-15 per person daily.
  5. Use comparison platforms — Always compare prices across multiple booking platforms to find the best deal.
